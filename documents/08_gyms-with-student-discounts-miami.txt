A real student discount on a gym membership should do two things: lower the monthly price and keep the rest of the deal honest. In Miami, most chain gyms get the first part half-right and the second part wrong on purpose. This guide breaks down what's actually offered, what's hidden, and where students should look depending on which campus they're on.
What "student discount" really means at the major Miami chains
First, the promotional hook. Most large chains advertise either a discounted student monthly rate or a $0-down promotional period for students. Both work the same way — they get you in the door at a sticker price that doesn't include what you'll actually pay over the term.
Planet Fitness
— "$10/month classic" or "$25 Black Card" sometimes with a college promotion. Not technically a student rate — anyone gets it. Comes with $49 signup fee, $49 annual maintenance fee in July, and a 12-month contract on Black Card. No "lunk alarm"-free zones (it's the whole gym).
Crunch Fitness
— Student promotions vary by location. Base prices $25–40/month. $50 signup, $49 annual fee, 12-month contract with 30-day cancellation window. Some locations offer reduced rates with a college ID; many don't.
LA Fitness
— Some Miami locations advertise student rates, others don't. Base $30–50/month. $99 signup, $49 annual fee, 12-month contract. Heavy peak-hour crowds (5–7 PM specifically).
EōS Fitness
— Aggressive $1-down student promos that auto-convert. $25–40/month after the promo. Contract-based. Newer equipment, sales-focused environment.
Equinox
— Does not offer a meaningful student rate. $250+/month, $200+ signup, 12-month contract.
The 12-month math that changes "cheap" student rates
Here's what nobody at the front desk shows you. A $25 "Black Card" sticker price at Planet Fitness over 12 months looks like $300. Add the $49 signup fee and the $49 annual maintenance charge that hits in July, and you're at $398. That's the real number — and you're locked in for the whole year. A $37/month no-contract gym with a $30 student rate ends up cheaper, more flexible, and easier to leave.
The 12-month commitment that affects students the most
Students have the most volatile schedules of any gym demographic. Semesters end, internships start in cities you don't live in, summers happen, financial aid disbursements vary, and unexpected life things hit. A 12-month gym contract is the wrong shape for that life.
Internship out of state for the summer? Still paying.
Going home for break? Still paying.
Class schedule changes and you can't get to the gym anymore? Still paying.
Cancel under the "30-day notice" clause? Still pay one more month after canceling.
Cancel after only 2 months? Often a $50–100 early termination fee plus the remaining months invoiced.
Campus-by-campus: where the actual options are
MDC InterAmerican Campus (627 SW 27th Ave, Little Havana)
By far the best student-gym situation in Miami. Gallo 8 Gym sits directly across SW 8th Street — a 2-minute walk. $30/month with any student ID, $5 day pass, no contract, bilingual front desk, free parking if you do drive. No other gym is closer to InterAmerican students.
MDC Wolfson Campus (Downtown — 300 NE 2nd Ave)
Downtown gym options are mostly chains and condo gyms. For a real student rate without a contract, Gallo 8 in Little Havana is 8–12 minutes away by car (free parking on arrival). Wolfson's own student rec center is also worth checking if you're full-time.
MDC Kendall + Hialeah + North Campuses
All three campuses sit in chain-dense neighborhoods. The nearest LA Fitness, Planet Fitness, and Crunch locations will sign you up at sticker price plus the fees. For a no-contract student rate, Gallo 8 is a 15–25 minute drive depending on which campus you're at — worth it for students staying longer than 6 months because the no-contract math wins.
FIU MMC Campus (11200 SW 8th St)
FIU has its own Wellness & Recreation Center included with tuition for enrolled students — start there. If you need a non-campus gym (FIU's hours don't always work, especially for grad students or anyone with a non-traditional schedule), Gallo 8 is straight east on SW 8th Street (about 8 miles, 20–25 min).
University of Miami (Coral Gables)
UM's Herbert Wellness Center is one of the best campus gyms in Florida and included for students. For supplementing with a community gym or training when off-campus, see our /coral-gables-gym page for route guidance from the UM/Gables area.
Barry University + St Thomas University
Both have campus rec centers for enrolled students. For off-campus training, both are 25–35 minutes from Gallo 8 — far enough that the trip only makes sense for students who live closer to Little Havana / central Miami than to their campus.
What to actually ask before signing up anywhere as a student
Is the student rate locked to the standard rate after I graduate, or only while I have an ID?
Is there a 12-month commitment attached to the student rate?
Is there a signup or enrollment fee — and does it apply to students?
Is there an annual or maintenance fee that hits in a specific month?
If I move home for the summer, what happens? Can I pause? Cancel without penalty?
Can I cancel at the front desk in person today, or is there a 30-day notice clause?
What's the day-pass price so I can try first?

Bottom line
If you're a student in Miami, the headline rate on a gym contract is almost never the real number. The real number is the rate × 12 + the signup fee + the annual fee + the cost of being locked in when your life changes mid-year. The honest student-discount gyms in Miami are independent, no-contract, and small enough to give you a straight answer at the front desk. For students on or near the MDC InterAmerican corridor, Gallo 8 Gym is the closest one — and for everyone else in Miami-Dade, it's usually the cheapest real-cost option once the chain fees clear.
